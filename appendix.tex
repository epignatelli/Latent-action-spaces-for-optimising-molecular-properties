\section{Codebook normalisation does not stabilise training at large codebook sizes}
In the scaling experiment, when the codebook size exceeds or is equal to the token vocabulary size, we observe training instability and performance degradation at lower losses.
The model effectively converged and the val loss starts going up.
To stabilise training and keep the model training for longer, we tried calculating the q loss of the commit loss of the vqvae on normalsised latents, rather than the raw latents, which are unbounded and can have large magnitudes that destabilise training.
However, with that, the model scaled way worse. We should report these results.
